# 🌍 Global Development Analytics Study

### Using World Bank and UNICEF Data

## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes global development indicators using data from the **World Bank** and **UNICEF** to understand how **public policies and government investments influence social and economic outcomes**.

The study focuses on identifying **patterns, correlations, and predictive insights** that can support **evidence-based policy decisions**.

The goal is to generate **cross-country comparative insights** that may help:

* Governments
* International organizations
* NGOs
* Development institutions

identify **effective policies to improve quality of life and human development**.

---

# 🎯 Strategic Research Questions

This study investigates key questions in **development economics and public policy**.

## Economy and Development

* Do countries with **higher public spending on education** have **better education quality**?
* Is there a relationship between **GDP per capita and life expectancy**?
* Does **GDP per capita growth** reduce **child mortality**?

## Public Policy

* Do countries that invest more in **education** have **lower child mortality**?
* Do countries with **high military expenditure** invest less in **education and nutrition**?

## Social Impact

How does **nutrition** influence:

* Child mortality
* Life expectancy

Is there a correlation between **total government expenditure** and **education quality**?

# 🔎 Analytical Framework

## 1️⃣ Cross-Country Comparison

The project creates **global rankings and comparative analysis** between countries.

Example:

| Country | GDP per Capita | Life Expectancy | Child Mortality |
| ------- | -------------- | --------------- | --------------- |
| Norway  | High           | High            | Low             |
| Brazil  | Medium         | Medium          | Medium          |
| Niger   | Low            | Low             | High            |

### Key Insight

While **wealthier countries tend to have better health outcomes**, **public policy decisions also play a critical role** in determining social development.

---

## 2️⃣ Investment vs Social Outcomes

One core objective is to analyze **how investments translate into societal results**.

### Example Analysis

**Education Expenditure vs Education Quality**

Methods used:

* Correlation analysis
* Regression modeling

Possible insight:

> Countries that invest more than **5% of GDP in education** tend to show **higher education quality on average**.

---

### Nutrition vs Child Mortality

Hypothesis:

> Better nutrition leads to **lower child mortality rates**.

Analytical methods:

* Linear regression
* Scatter plot analysis
* Clustering techniques

---

## 3️⃣ Social Development Index (SDI)

The project introduces a **composite indicator** to measure overall development.

Example formula:

```
SDI =
0.25 * normalized(life_expectancy)
+ 0.25 * normalized(education_quality)
+ 0.25 * normalized(nutrition)
- 0.25 * normalized(child_mortality)
```

This index allows the creation of **global development rankings**.
